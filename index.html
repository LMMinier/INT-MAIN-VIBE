Markets / Space Corrected

SpaceX Goes Public — And Mints The First Trillionaire

Dispatch 01 · June 12, 2026 · @wheresmaicol
CORRECTION: an earlier figure listed the raise at $85.7 billion. That was wrong. The accurate numbers: ~$75B raised at a $1.75T valuation.

On June 12, 2026, SpaceX listed on the Nasdaq under the ticker SPCX, priced at $135 a share, raising roughly $75 billion at a $1.75 trillion valuation. That makes it the largest IPO in history, surpassing Saudi Aramco's 2019 record. It is not a "valuation target" or a deal that might close — it closed.

Shares climbed 19% on day one, pushing the company's market cap past roughly $2.1 trillion. The pop did something no IPO had ever done before: it pushed Elon Musk's paper net worth past the line, making him the world's first trillionaire.

The strategic read matters more than the headline number. SpaceX going public at this scale, in the same month the four hyperscalers committed ~$650B to AI infrastructure, confirms that the capital markets now treat compute, launch capacity, and energy as a single strategic asset class. Starlink's subscriber base and launch cadence turned space access from a government program into a commercial utility — and the filing flagged water scarcity as a material operating risk for the data-center infrastructure SpaceX's ambitions increasingly touch.

SRC: Intellectia.AI · exchange filings, June 12, 2026
AI / Policy Verified

Fable 5 Didn't Get "Restricted." It Got Shut Off.

Dispatch 02 · June 12–15, 2026 · @wheresmaicol
REFRAME: "in high-stakes talks to resolve export curbs" undersells it. Talks to avoid restrictions → talks to undo a global shutdown that already happened.

On June 12, the US issued an export-control directive suspending all access to Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 by any foreign national — inside or outside the United States, including Anthropic's own foreign-national employees. This was not a warning or a pending review.

Anthropic's response was to shut both models down globally rather than operate a fractured, nationality-gated service. Other Claude models — Opus, Sonnet, Haiku — remain operational; Mythos API calls reroute to Opus 4.8. So the negotiations now underway are not to pre-empt a restriction. They are to reverse a shutdown that is already in force.

The precedent is the story. An export order landed within 72 hours of a commercial AI launch, on a jailbreak Anthropic says produced no harmful results and no Mythos-specific uplift. "No demonstrated harm" was not a sufficient defense once the model was treated as a dual-use technology. Every frontier lab now has to model a scenario where its most capable product is switched off by government order days after release.

SRC: Al Jazeera · Crypto Briefing · Anthropic statements, June 12–15, 2026
Policy / Platforms Verified — announced, not enacted

Britain Moves To Bar Under-16s From Social Media

Dispatch 03 · June 15, 2026 · @wheresmaicol
STATUS: do not write "passed." Law passed → policy announced June 15, legislation not yet enacted.

On June 15, Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced a plan to ban under-16s from social media, explicitly following Australia's model. The proposal covers Snapchat, TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, and X — but pointedly excludes WhatsApp, Signal, and YouTube Kids, drawing a line between broadcast feeds and private messaging.

The distinction to hold onto: this is an announced policy, not enacted law. There is no statute yet, no enforcement mechanism, and no commencement date. Britain has signaled intent and named the platforms; it has not legislated.

If it does become law, it would be the largest Western jurisdiction after Australia to wall minors off from the major feeds — and it would force the same age-verification questions that have stalled similar efforts elsewhere. Who verifies age, how, and at what privacy cost is the unsolved core of every one of these bans.

SRC: NPR, June 15, 2026
M&A / Enterprise AI Verified

Salesforce Buys Fin — The Company Formerly Known As Intercom — For $3.6B

Dispatch 04 · June 2026 · @wheresmaicol

Salesforce is acquiring Fin, the AI customer-service company formerly known as Intercom, for $3.6 billion. The rebrand from Intercom to Fin — the name of its AI support agent — tells you exactly where the value moved: from a human-staffed help-desk product to an autonomous agent that resolves tickets without a person in the loop.

This is the jobs story (Dispatch on the disappearing first job) expressed as a balance sheet. Tier-1 support is already ~67% automated at major platforms; Salesforce paying $3.6B to own a leading agent is the enterprise market pricing in that the entry-level support role is not coming back. The acquisition folds Fin into Salesforce's agentic stack alongside its existing customer-service cloud.

SRC: deal reporting, June 2026
Funding / India Verified

India's Newest Unicorn: Sarvam AI Raises $234M

Dispatch 05 · June 2026 · @wheresmaicol

Sarvam AI has raised $234 million — led by HCLTech's $150M cheque — at a $1.5 billion valuation, making it India's newest AI unicorn. The HCLTech anchor is notable: a legacy IT-services giant putting nine figures into a sovereign-model startup is the old Indian tech economy funding the new one.

Sarvam's pitch is sovereignty — building large language models tuned for Indian languages and data, rather than depending on US frontier labs whose most capable models can be switched off by US export order (see Dispatch 02). In a year defined by compute concentration in three US zip codes, a well-funded sovereign-AI push from the world's most populous country is a structural counter-move, not just a funding round.
