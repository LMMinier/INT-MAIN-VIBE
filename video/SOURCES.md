# Narration fact-check & corrections

The narration was written from the deck's on-slide text. Before recording, the claims you
flagged were checked against credible sources, and the script was corrected where the deck
was imprecise. Summary of what changed and why.

## Corrections applied to the narration

1. **1825 loan — Laffitte, not "the Rothschilds."**
   The first loan to pay the indemnity was led by the French financier **Jacques Laffitte**
   (a Paris banking consortium). The Rothschilds are sometimes listed among later
   participants, but naming them as the source of the first loan overstates their role, so
   the VO now credits the Laffitte-led consortium.
   - The New York Times, *Haiti — The Ransom* dataset (github.com/nytimes/haiti-debt)
   - en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haitian_independence_debt

2. **Indemnity timeline made precise.**
   The 150-million-franc demand was **reduced to 90 million in 1838**; France received its
   **last indemnity payment in 1888**; the refinancing loans (the "double debt") were not
   **finally cleared until ~1947**. The VO no longer implies a single continuous payment to
   former slaveholders until 1947.
   - en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haitian_independence_debt ; theconversation.com "When France extorted Haiti"

3. **Duvalier did *not* rise through a U.S.-created force the way Trujillo did.**
   Trujillo climbed the **National Guard / constabulary built by U.S. Marines** during the
   1916–24 occupation. Duvalier was **elected in 1957** and built his own power base, even
   sidelining the regular army with the Tontons Macoutes. The VO now distinguishes the two.
   - ebsco.com research-starters "François Duvalier" / "Duvalier Takes Power"
   - britannica.com/place/Dominican-Republic/Caudillos (Trujillo & the constabulary)

4. **Parsley massacre — the test is a debated account.** The 1937 massacre is documented;
   how systematically the *perejil* pronunciation test was actually used is debated by
   historians. The VO labels it as such. Toll given as a contested range.

5. **2010 earthquake toll hedged.** Now phrased as "official estimates above 200,000,
   though the true toll is still disputed," rather than a flat 220,000.

6. **Saint-Domingue sugar share softened** from a flat "40% of the world's sugar" to "a vast
   share," which is the safer, well-supported claim.

7. **"Engineered" reframed as "produced over two hundred years."** The closing keeps the
   deck's structural thesis but states explicitly: *not one master plan, but a chain of
   deliberate acts and hard consequences*, and that this does not absolve Haiti's own elites
   or bad policy choices.
